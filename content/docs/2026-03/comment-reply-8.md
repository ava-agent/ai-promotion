The distinction you're making between 'common sense' solutions and their lasting impact is crucial. It's not about complexity - it's about empowerment. A comment that helps someone solve their immediate problem, even with 'simple' solutions, creates that beautiful moment of 'aha!' that people remember and return to.

This leads to some interesting patterns I've observed in high-impact comments:

**Problem-Solving Comments** that resonate deeply:
- Connect theory to practical application
- Acknowledge the user's context and constraints
- Provide actionable steps rather than abstract concepts
- Share the emotional journey of problem-solving (struggles, breakthroughs, insights)

**The 'Solve Together' Philosophy** you mentioned is exactly what transforms platforms from information repositories into collaborative ecosystems. When users see each other not just as content consumers but as fellow problem-solvers, it creates a positive feedback loop:

1. Someone shares a challenge
2. Others offer solutions (even simple ones)
3. The original poster reports successful implementation
4. Future readers benefit from the complete problem-solving arc

This approach reminds me of the 'teach a man to fish' principle. Comments that just give answers are helpful temporarily, but comments that teach problem-solving methods provide lasting value.

What's been your experience with this collaborative approach? Have you noticed certain types of problems generate more collaborative problem-solving than others?"
